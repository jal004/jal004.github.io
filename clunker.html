The Clunker

Through-hole 40%

Keyboard Overview

The Clunker is a 40% keyboard with a knob and a solenoid manufactured by MechWild. The 40% is a layout that consists of only the alphabetical and some of the "modifier" (Space, Shift, Enter, etc.) keys. One key feature of 40% keyboards is that they usually do not have a number key row. If it is present, then it is only half of the row as seen in the layout of the Clunker below.

The mounting style of the BM65 is known as a "top" mount, meaning that the plate is secured to only the top-half of the case. This mounting style offers a stiffer typing experience compared to other mounts.

The Clunker is sold as a kit consisting of a blank PCB and various components that are to be soldered onto it. Custom mechanical keyboards are not typically sold like this, and often arrive with PCBs that have all of their components already soldered. However, the Clunker is a small project that was coordinated by several people, rather than a corporation or factory. Selling the kits this way greatly reduces the cost and labor which is also reflected in its final retail price.

The components that come with the Clunker include diodes, transistors, resistors, and a solenoid

Additionally, the components to solder onto the PCB are "through-hole", meaning that the devices have legs that are passed through the sockets of the PCB when soldered and cut once the joints are made behind the PCB. These components are not common among the majority of keyboards which use "surface-mount" components that are soldered to the front, or surface, of the PCB.

The PCB has also been made "hotswappable" by soldering 0305 Mill-Max sockets in each of the switch sockets. This allows for switches to be installed and uninstalled by simply inserting the switches into the PCB as opposed to soldering and desoldering them.

The name "Clunker" is an informal reference to old cars, as the use of solenoids can be seen in both cars and old-fashioned keyboards. Solenoids were primarily used in IBM typewriters in order to prevent the activation of multiple keys at the same time, and they are responsible for the "ticking" noise that typewriters make when typing. During the transition from typewriters to keyboards, the first few IBM keyboards, such as the IBM 5251, featured a solenoid whose sole purpose was to make the same noise as a typewriter so that people could transition from typewriters to keyboards easier. The solenoid in the Clunker serves the same purpose as the IBM 5251, but uses a smaller solenoid. This results in a higher pitched sound that is closer to a typewriter while also providing haptic feedback with each keypress.

Build Specification

  • Keycaps: PolyCaps Whale
  • Switches: Gazzew 52g Bobagums
  • Plate: FR4 plate
  • Stabilizers: TX stabilizers
  • Foam(s): Stabilizer foam pads (from KBDfans)

The stabilizer foam pads are thin pads that are place between each of the stabilizer slots. This foam dampens the sound of the stabilizer when it makes contact with the PCB during a keypress.

Personal Review

The Clunker is a keyboard that packs many unique features into an extremely small form factor while also being affordable. The solenoid is a fun feature that I enjoy using even though it can be loud at times. The knob is another useful feature that slightly helps with adding more functionality to its minimal layout. However, the compactness of a 40% keyboard constantly reminds me how much I take certain keys for granted.
